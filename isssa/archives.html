Spatial Disparities in Urban Healthcare Infrastructure: A Case Study of the Varanasi District

Dr. A. R. Siddiqui, Shaurya Singh
Abstract: This paper investigates the uneven distribution of healthcare facilities across the urban and peri-urban sectors of the Varanasi district. Utilizing GIS mapping and demographic overlays, the study identifies critical "medical deserts" where population density severely outpaces available clinical resources. The findings suggest immediate policy interventions in regional planning to ensure equitable health access...

Evaluating the Economic Impact of Micro-Financing on Rural Agricultural Development in Eastern UP

Prof. D. K. Singh, Neha Sharma
Abstract: Micro-financing has been touted as a revolutionary tool for rural upliftment. This empirical study analyzes survey data from 400 farming households in Eastern Uttar Pradesh to measure the direct correlation between micro-credit access and agricultural yield improvements. Results indicate a 22% increase in crop diversity among loan recipients, though debt-trap risks remain a critical concern...
