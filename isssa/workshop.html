Context & Rationale

The integration of Artificial Intelligence and modern digital tools is no longer an option, but a necessity for modern academia.

This 5-Day intensive National Workshop is designed to bridge the digital divide. Under the guidelines of the National Education Policy (NEP) 2020, participants will receive hands-on training in Generative AI for academic writing, digital classroom management, advanced data analysis tools, and maintaining research integrity in the era of automated content.

Who Should Attend?
  • Faculty Members & Assistant Professors
  • Ph.D. Research Scholars
  • EdTech Developers & Curriculum Designers
  • Academic Administrators

Core Learning Modules

15+ Hours of interactive, hands-on training delivered by industry experts and senior academicians.

Generative AI in Pedagogy

Mastering ChatGPT, Gemini, and Claude for creating dynamic lesson plans, case studies, and interactive quizzes.

AI for Literature Review

Utilizing modern tools like Elicit, Consensus, and Mendeley for rapid literature mapping and citation management.

LMS & Digital Classrooms

Setting up and automating Google Workspace for Education, Canvas, and interactive whiteboarding tools.

Spatial Tools & Remote Sensing

Integrating open-source GIS tools and spatial data visualization into social science curriculum and field research.

Ethics & Anti-Plagiarism

Navigating AI ethics, deepfake detection, and utilizing advanced similarity checking software (Turnitin/DrillBit).

Automated Assessments

Designing auto-grading rubrics, proctored digital exams, and tracking student analytics in real-time.

Workshop Itinerary

Day 1 | May 25

Inauguration & Fundamentals of AI

Keynote address by ICSSR delegates. Introduction to the evolution of Educational Technology and prompt engineering basics for educators.

Day 2 | May 26

Modern Research & Data Tools

Hands-on sessions on automated literature reviews, data visualization, and integrating spatial/GIS mapping into non-technical research methodologies.

Day 3 | May 27

LMS & Assessment Automation

Designing interactive digital classrooms, deploying automated grading rubrics, and securing online assessments against AI-assisted cheating.

Day 4 & 5 | May 28 - 29

Ethics, Practicum & Valedictory

Participant project presentations, discussions on AI ethics and copyright policies, followed by the valedictory session and certificate distribution at Shri Baldev P.G. College.
