1. Aims and Scope

The Indian National Journal of Advanced Social Science Studies (INJASSS), published under the aegis of the International Social Sciences Studies Association (ISSSA), accepts high-quality, unpublished, and original research spanning the diverse domains of social sciences.

We specifically encourage submissions addressing geographical spatial planning, human resource development, political governance, economic strategies, and socio-demographic shifts. Research that bridges the gap between theoretical frameworks and real-world policy application is highly favored.

2. Types of Manuscripts Accepted

Authors may submit their work under one of the following categories. Please indicate the category during the submission process:

  • Original Research Articles: Full-length empirical or theoretical papers presenting new data and findings. (Length: 3,000 – 6,000 words)
  • Review Papers: Comprehensive, critical evaluations of existing literature on a specific, highly relevant social science topic. (Length: 4,000 – 7,000 words)
  • Case Studies: In-depth analyses of specific regional phenomena, policy implementations, or institutional frameworks. (Length: 2,500 – 4,000 words)
  • Short Communications: Brief reports outlining preliminary findings from ongoing large-scale research projects. (Length: 1,500 – 2,500 words)

Word Count Policy

The stated word limits are strictly inclusive of the abstract, main text, and footnotes. References and appendices are excluded from the final word count.

3. General Formatting Requirements

To facilitate the editorial and peer-review process, all manuscripts must adhere to the following typographical and formatting guidelines. Failure to comply may result in immediate desk rejection.

  • File Format: Submissions must be uploaded as Microsoft Word documents (.doc or .docx). PDFs will not be accepted for the initial review stage.
  • Font & Size: The entire manuscript must be typed in Times New Roman, 12-point font.
  • Spacing: The text should be 1.5 spaced throughout, including the abstract and references.
  • Margins: Maintain standard 1-inch (2.54 cm) margins on all sides of an A4 page layout.
  • Language: Manuscripts must be written in clear, grammatically correct US or UK English. Authors for whom English is a second language are strongly encouraged to utilize professional academic proofreading services prior to submission.

4. Manuscript Structure

To guarantee the integrity of our Double-Blind Peer Review process, authors must structure their documents carefully to separate identifying information from the core research.

4.1. Anonymization (Crucial Step)

The manuscript file uploaded to our portal must not contain any author names, affiliations, or identifying metadata. Do not include a title page in the primary document. You will provide your author details exclusively through the digital forms on our submission portal.

4.2. Main Text Sequence

Your uploaded document should follow this sequence:

  1. Title: Centered, bold, Title Case, maximum 15 words.
  2. Abstract: A concise summary of the research background, methodology, results, and conclusion. Maximum 250 words. Do not use citations in the abstract.
  3. Keywords: Provide 4 to 6 keywords separated by commas directly beneath the abstract.
  4. Introduction: Establish the context, literature gap, and explicit objectives of the study.
  5. Methodology: Detail the study area, data collection procedures, statistical tools, and analytical frameworks utilized.
  6. Results & Discussion: Present findings logically. Discuss the implications of the results in relation to existing literature and the original hypothesis.
  7. Conclusion: Summarize the key findings, limitations of the study, and recommendations for future research or policy.

5. Citations and References

INJASSS utilizes standardized referencing formats to maintain academic uniformity. Authors may choose between APA (American Psychological Association, 7th Edition) or MLA (Modern Language Association, 9th Edition). Whichever style is chosen must be applied consistently throughout the manuscript.

In-Text Citation Examples (APA)

Single Author: (Singh, 2025)
Two Authors: (Nag & Siddiqui, 2024)
Three or more: (Prasad et al., 2023)

Reference List: Place the reference list at the end of the document. Entries should be alphabetized by the last name of the first author. Ensure that every in-text citation has a corresponding entry in the reference list, and vice versa. Include DOIs (Digital Object Identifiers) for all electronic journal articles where available.

6. Tables, Figures, and Spatial Maps

Visual data representations are highly encouraged, particularly for geography and economics submissions. Follow these formatting rules:

  • Placement: Insert tables and figures directly into the text where they are first mentioned, rather than grouping them at the end of the document.
  • Numbering: Number all tables (e.g., Table 1) and figures (e.g., Figure 1) consecutively.
  • Captions: Table captions should be placed above the table. Figure/Map captions should be placed below the image.
  • Quality: Maps generated via GIS software and data charts must be high resolution (minimum 300 DPI). Ensure text within images is legible.
  • Sourcing: If secondary data is used to create a table or map, the exact source must be cited immediately below the visual element.

7. Ethical Policies & Plagiarism

ISSSA and the INJASSS Editorial Board are committed to upholding the highest standards of publication ethics, aligning with the guidelines set forth by the Committee on Publication Ethics (COPE).

7.1. Originality and Plagiarism

All submissions are subjected to automated similarity checks using industry-standard software (e.g., Turnitin/iThenticate). The maximum acceptable overall similarity index is 10%, with no more than 2% from any single source. Submissions exceeding this threshold will be desk-rejected.

7.2. Simultaneous Submission

Manuscripts must not be under consideration, accepted for publication, or already published elsewhere. Authors found engaging in simultaneous submission will face a permanent ban from the journal.

7.3. Authorship & Conflict of Interest

All listed authors must have made a significant intellectual contribution to the research. During submission, the corresponding author must declare any financial or personal relationships with other people or organizations that could inappropriately influence (bias) their work.

8. The Peer Review Process

We pride ourselves on a rigorous, transparent, and constructive evaluation process.

  1. Initial Desk Review (7-10 Days): The Editorial Secretary checks the manuscript for alignment with the journal's scope, formatting compliance, and plagiarism.
  2. Double-Blind Peer Review (4-6 Weeks): Qualifying manuscripts are anonymized and assigned to at least two independent subject-matter experts from our Advisory Council (e.g., Prof. P. Nag, Prof. A. R. Siddiqui).
  3. Editorial Decision: Based on the reviewers' feedback, the Editor will issue one of four decisions: Accept, Minor Revisions, Major Revisions, or Reject.
  4. Author Tracking: Authors can view their real-time status and download secure reviewer feedback via the INJASSS Author Tracking Portal using their unique Tracking ID.
