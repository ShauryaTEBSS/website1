Executive Portfolios Up for Election

President

The highest office of the society. Presides over all General Body Meetings and represents ISSSA at national and international academic consortiums.

Vice President

Assists the President in society affairs and assumes presidential duties in their absence. Oversees specialized regional sub-committees.

General Secretary

The administrative backbone of the society. Manages the INJASSS publication pipeline, handles official correspondence, and organizes annual seminars.

Treasurer

Responsible for the society's financial health, managing membership dues, seminar budgets, and presenting the annual financial audit.

Joint Executive Members (4 Seats)

Four elected members who assist the core committee in organizing events, expanding the membership base, and conducting initial peer-review desk screenings.

Electoral Process & Rules

The Election Timeline

  • 1

    Call for Nominations

    The Election Commission issues a formal notification to all registered Life Members 45 days prior to the election date.

  • 2

    Filing & Scrutiny

    Candidates submit their nomination papers, proposed and seconded by two existing Life Members. Papers are scrutinized for validity.

  • 3

    Withdrawal Period

    Candidates are given a 7-day window to voluntarily withdraw their nominations before the final ballot is printed.

  • 4

    Secret Ballot Voting

    Voting is conducted physically via a secret ballot box during the General Body Meeting. Proxy voting is not permitted.

  • Declaration of Results

    Votes are counted immediately post-election by an independent Returning Officer, and the new EC is announced.

Eligibility Criteria

To Vote (Electoral College):
  • Must be a registered "Professional Life Member" of ISSSA.
  • Student and Institutional members do not hold voting rights.
  • Membership must be approved at least 30 days prior to the election notification.
To Run for Office (Candidate):
  • Must meet all criteria for voting.
  • Must have been a continuous Life Member in good standing for a minimum of three (3) years.
  • Must not have been convicted of academic misconduct or plagiarism by any recognized university or journal board.
  • For the post of President, the candidate must hold a Doctorate (Ph.D.) and currently hold or have held the rank of an Associate Professor or higher.
Code of Conduct: Campaigning is limited to academic achievements and vision for the society. Defamation of other candidates will lead to immediate disqualification.
